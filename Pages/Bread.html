Breaking Bread

Butter, flour, soy wax, oil, linen, clear acrylic, sound, brass bowl.

What spaces ignite our shared humanity? Who pays the price for our collective appetites?

Breaking Bread unfolds as both installation and performance, an invocation of communal rituals, fragmented selves, and the cycles of consumption and decay. At its core, the work reimagines the table as an altar, a space where bodies gather in both communion and disintegration. The installation recalls the 1977 Bread Riots in Egypt, when the sudden removal of state subsidies exposed the precarity of survival, turning bread from a daily staple into a contested symbol of power. In a moment of rupture, food was no longer just sustenance—it became leverage, withheld and granted at will. To consume is to participate in an economy of control.

Cast bread sculptures of hardened butter dissolve under touch, leaving behind a residue of excess and erosion. Overhead, a series of butter knives form a fragile threshold, one that is dismantled with each blade taken, carving into the remains. With every gesture, the space shifts between ritual and rupture, the collective and the solitary.

Activated through a performance by Assi Abogado, the work lingers in the tension between indulgence and restraint, inheritance and disavowal. Over 20 minutes, the performance escalates in aggression as it delves into the themes of vanity and consumption, set against a meticulously deliberate pace. It is an offering and a reckoning, a meditation on what is taken, what is left behind, and what ultimately remains.

Breaking Bread was commissioned by Jameel Art Centre as part of their 2024 Youth Takeover Program.

Accompanied by a sound piece by Assi Abogado, Nour Elbery, Marwan Ali, and Abdulrahman Hassanin. Film Edited by Ailime Branco


Marwan Ali

Marwan Ali is an Egyptian architect based in Dubai and a graduate of the American University of Sharjah. His work explores speculative cartographies, narrative reconstruction, and the shifting relationship between land, memory, and displacement. Engaging with mapping as an act of interpretation rather than definition, he examines how histories slip through fixed boundaries and how gaps in the archive might be reimagined.

Marwan’s approach blends tradition with contemporary methodologies, drawing from the region’s architectural heritage while interrogating its transformations. He was awarded special mention at the Architizer Vision Awards 2023 and won the Christo and Jeanne-Claude Award in 2020. He has collaborated on projects across the Middle East, engaging space as both a physical and narrative construct, shaping architecture as a site of negotiation between presence and absence.

Nour Elbery

Nour Elbery is an Egyptian artist, architect, and curator currently based in Dubai. She holds a Bachelor of Architecture from the American University of Sharjah (B.Arch 2022). Her work examines spatial conditions that anticipate and regulate the body, exploring how architectures dictate movement, enforce thresholds, and generate moments of misalignment. Spanning installation and sculpture, she interrogates the mechanics of spatial control—surfaces that instruct, structures that persist even in dysfunction—while exposing the fractures within these environments, revealing moments of hesitation, misregistration, and resistance.

Her work has been exhibited at Jameel Arts Center for the installation Breaking Bread, commissioned as part of the Youth Takeover Program 2024. She was part of the winning trio to receive The Christo and Jeanne-Claude Award (2021) for the installation Cocoon.
