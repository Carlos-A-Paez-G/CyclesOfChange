Eden: Decay and Bloom

As our lives become increasingly disconnected from nature, drawn toward technology and artificial spaces, Eden: Decay and Bloom seeks to restore this connection through live sound and text in an open setting. Featuring French compositions inspired by the landscapes of the Arab world, the program reflects a shared human relationship with nature that transcends borders and cultures. At times dissonant and unpredictable, the music serves as a reminder that nature is not only a source of harmony and grounding but also a powerful, untamed force.

Presented in an outdoor space, Eden highlights the irreplaceable energy of live performance as music, nature, and human presence converge in real time. Eden reminds us of the harmonious relationship between sound and nature as well as the unpredictability of that partnership. It is a meditation on cycles - of growth and erosion, harmony and dissonance - reminding us that Eden can be both a sanctuary and an exile. Just as the Eden of scripture offered shelter before casting humanity into the unknown, this Eden invites the opportunity to reflect on nature’s dual role: a place of refuge and reckoning.


Charles McGlone

Charles McGlone is a tenor based between London and Dubai. He is currently studying with Sophie Grimmer. In 2022, Charles graduated from Trinity Laban Conservatoire in London. There he premiered Edward Jessen’s ‘Syllable’ under the direction of Joseph Alford (Royal Opera House) and the baton of Gregory Rose (London Philharmonic, the Royal Scottish National Orchestra, BBC Concert Orchestra). Performance experience in the UK started at Grange Park Opera with performances in Puccini’s “Tosca” and Wagner’s “Tristan und Isolde” followed by Lord Lechery in “The Pilgrims Progress” with British Youth Opera at the Three Choirs Festival in 2023. Charles has enjoyed masterclasses with Barbara Hannigan, James Clutton (Opera Holland Park), and two with Nicky Spence. With a keen interest in collaboration, he has worked with Streetwise Opera and composer Mathieu Robert to commission ‘Mo Li Hua’, a song inspired by Chinese folk tradition.

Stanislav Fedyuk

Stanislav Fedyuk gained his Master’s in piano at the Lviv Music Academy in Ukraine, specializing in performance, accompaniment and chamber ensemble and gained his Licentiate Diploma with Distinction from Trinity College of London in performance. He has participated in numerous national and international competitions in Ukraine winning 1st, 2nd and other special prizes both as a solo pianist and accompanist. Stan has performed across Europe and the Middle East, playing various music styles including pop, rock, jazz and musical theatre and shared the stage with Diana Krall, Elvis Costello, Maverick Sabre and Sir Dirk Brosse. He has had the honour to perform for members of the UAE Royal Family, as well as Buzz Aldrin, Edgar Davies and Quincy Jones, who acknowledged Stan for his piano services in Dubai. Recent performance highlights include many shows for the world famous Candlelight concerts and at venues such as Expo and Coca-Cola Arena. Stan is also an sexperienced accompanist, educator and adjudicator in Dubai and across the Middle East, collaborating with opera, jazz and pop singers as well as performing for universities, embassies, competitions and masterclasses in the Middle East and Europe.
