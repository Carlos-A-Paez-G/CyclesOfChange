INSIDE

INSIDE is an on-going work-in-progress that studies performative embodied geometries. The performance experiments with the relationship between moving bodies and a tailored seven-meter diameter fabric spatial apparatus. When activated by the body, the fabric apparatus transforms two and three dimensionally redefining the space of performance and creating a co-dependence with the performers. The soundscape, also generated by the body in different states of action and stasis, layers recorded and live sounds during the performance and uses the natural biological cyclical rhythm of breath as co-performer. The experience sets up a tension between audience, performer, and environment, that blurs the space of performance. INSIDE is a speculative work that questions what constitutes being inside – or outside – the space of performance.

Ksenija Kovač-Romano

Ksenija Kovač-Romano is a professional international ballet soloist, renowned choreographer, and certified ballet instructor based in Abu Dhabi, UAE and Ljubljana, Slovenia. She has over 25 years of performance and teaching experience in classical and modern ballet in Ballet and Opera productions. She has worked for and collaborated with internationally renowned choreographers and theatre directors such as Irek Mukhamedov, Leo Mujic, Julio Lopez, Youri Vamos, Gagik Ismailian, Jiri Kylian, Heinz Spoerli, Dragan Živadinov, Hans van Manen, and Raimondo Rebeck. She has performed in 47 different professional productions in the US, UK, Russia, Germany, Austria, Croatia, the Netherlands, and Slovenia. In addition to her professional dance career, she has represented professional ballet dancers in her role as National Committee Member in the Trade Union of Culture and Nature for Slovenia. In the UAE, she is a ballet and contemporary dance teacher and choreographer for Berklee Abu Dhabi and a member of the PerForm program at the Cultural Foundation Abu Dhabi. Kovač-Romano is a 2024 Numoo Artist, The Arts Center at NYU Abu Dhabi’s professional development program for performance artists supported by the U.S. mission to the UAE. She is the founder and artistic director of YaxenDance, an international ballet and contemporary dance company.

Tania Ursomarzo

Tania Ursomarzo is an architect, multidisciplinary designer, material fabrication specialist, and educator based in Sharjah and New York City. Immersed somewhere between architecture, design, art, fashion, and performance, her interdisciplinary approach to creative practice centers on the design and fabrication of space and objects in response to the body and the hybridization of analogue and digital technologies. Recent projects include ENSIAB, an installation made from soy wax masonry for Dubai Design Week 2024 and Study for an Incision, a large site-specific light mapping of 18 cycles of shadow in Alserkal Avenue (Dubai) for the 2021 Yard Commission awarded by the Alserkal Arts Foundation. Ursomarzo is Assistant Professor of Architecture and Interior Design at the American University of Sharjah. She is the founder and creative director of ETCETERAcreative, an experimental design research studio, and TRIPTYCH, an alternative fashion product brand. Creative works range from installations, multimedia art, exhibition and performance space design, architecture and interior design, to product design and fashion product. Over her 25 plus years of professional international experience her work has been built, exhibited, screened, performed, published, and received awards in the US, Canada, Europe, Asia, and the Middle East.
